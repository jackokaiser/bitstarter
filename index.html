Explaining something to someone isn't a straightforward task. Ever found yourself mimicking with your hands to picture a concept? I believe those gestures are important. They enhance the communication flow. Altough, moving hands in the air might not be sufficient.
What if we could directly transplant an idea from a brain to another? Well, this is not yet possible. But what we can do instead, is transplant the idea from a brain to a computer, and then transplant it back to another brain.

Handy Sketcher is a 3D Sketching tool that relies on the Leap Motion Controller.
If you can picture your idea in your mind, then mimicking it with your hands should be enough to output a 3D sketch of your thougts. I'll do the best to provide you with online easy-to-use features.

FAQ

What is the Leap Motion Controller?
It is a small device that tracks your hands and fingers position precisly such that you can interact with your computer in natural ways.
It is quite new and started shipping on July 22.
Who am I?
I'm 22 years old French citizen, that just completed its MSc in Computer Science. I rencently got interested into startup as I started working for Skimlab. Before that, I've been working in Strasbourg research lab and I was quite interested into Computer Graphics. I now put effort into combining both worlds and developping real graphic products.
What do I need to install?
Nothing, it is an online app. Though, you'd need up-to-date graphic drivers and a web browser that supports webGL. Moreover you also need a Leap Motion Controller to use the app.
